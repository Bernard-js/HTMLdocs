Displaying video text tracks

Introduction to WebVTT

some people will need to read text transcriptions of the audio tracks in some video files, for example:

  • many people are deaf or have some scale of auditory impairments.
  • others might be in a noisy enviroment, like a bar.
  • In the same way, people may be in an enviroment where the audio can cause a distraction, like a library.
  • People who don't understand the language spoken in the video might want a text transcript or a translation of the audio.

Thanks to HTML5, it's possible to write text strings along with additional metadata, like specific times that the text should be displayed. Those text strings are called cues , and there are many cues that can be used to video files, such as:

Subtitles
Translation of the audio tracks, used mostly by foreign people.
Captions
Synchronized transcriptions or dialogs, To let people that can't hear the audio understand what's going on.
Timed descriptions
Text that should be spoken by the media player to let blind or visually impaired people understand what's going on.

to do so, we use a file format called WebVTT, in which the captions will be at. we use a <track> element, as a child of the video element and after the source tags.
